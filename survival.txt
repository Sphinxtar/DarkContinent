                             Linus Sphinx's
                             Dark Continent
                             Survival Manual

          An Environmentally Safe Game For Intrepid Adventurers

INTRODUCTION
You are alone with no way back to civilization in the deepest darkest jungle
filled with predators, perils and pitfalls. It's the law of the jungle that
only the strong survive and kill or be killed as you fight the jungle and all
it's inhabitants for the right to live and in that savage instinct strive to
be king of this brutal land in which fate has cast you adrift.

HINTS
Your players strength is built up by eating, mangoes, bananas and dead
animals. Strength loss comes from moving, getting hit by animals, other
players, traps. How much strength is lost by movement is controlled by
thirst and hunger, the less you eat the faster you lose strength, the type
of terrain you're on is the other big factor.  How easy the game is for your
player is controlled by your level of jungle savvy, when your strength
divided by your savvy level is greater than 2500, ie your gain a point of
savvy at 2500, 5000, 7500, 10,000 12,500 etc. The rock throwing, hitting of
opponents, beast etc. gets easier and does more damage the higher your savvy.
You will also gain points as you gain items necessary to win the game. When
you first start use your swiss army knife and your compass, next move one
space SOUTH, you will find more important items on the ground there. INSPECT
everything, each has clues for it's USE. USING items is how to overcome the
pitfalls, maintain your players health etc.  All items have some usage
somewhere, sometime.  You may wish to jot down where you bury things with the
shovel, where you've laid empty spam can traps etc. to supplement the MAP
commands information.  If your leg is broken U)se a stick of bamboo to splint
it. If you should luck out and find the amulet, inspect it and then use it to
free an extra space in your bag.

PLAY
The game takes place on a grid numbered 0-39 east/west and 0-59 north/south.
You are given a map and a few items to inspect and use and you will find a
few more scattered in front of or behind you on the ground.  Your map is weak
at first since the continent is completely uncharted but as you move the
water holes, mango trees, snake pits, tiger traps, quicksand, bamboo groves,
are all charted but NOT where you bury things, set spam traps etc.
Tip: Don't bother marking the plane wreck location it changes every night and
it's cargo of medicine and canned meat are refilled.  Your prompt line will
keep you informed of your main statistics and you recieve a running commentary
on your condition as your progress becomes impeded or you grow weak. When your
strength hits a level of only ten moves left with your current hunger and
thirst level you will start to black out, bloat etc.  If you run your character
into the ground he will pass out and maybe die, not only ending your turn but
leave easy pickings for the other bwanas.  A script is kept for your character
to move on while you are out and will stir you from sleep and cause you to
attack or run from another player, your hunter will only obey this as long as
you have strength, can't penetrate high cliffs and will stop at the edge of the
world. If your character suffers from a broken leg, it will only be able to
move one space at a turn, in any encounter with a live player it's your script
reading players turn first so use it to your advantage. Your strength etc. will
be adjusted the same as if you were playing live so leave your bwana in good
health and they'll be able to take care of themselves, it' a good idea if you
know in advance where your character will go, which brings us back to checking
the map again.  The other players can run your bwana down til he's too tired to
move or defend themselves, so notice the powers of the look command and use
the terrain to find an advantageous hiding/sleeping spot. Your character
will keep a full log of all intruders and resulting interplay which you will
be shown at your next entry to the continent.

Your main prompt line consists of:

 strength     thirst     prompts for input are always marked so.
      |        /                       |
 :-( 2000 50 100 50:12 What now bwana? )-:
          |        |
        hunger     map location IF you have a compass and can see it.

Yes or no questions default to uppercase choice like this?:-(Y/n)-:
In the above example hitting return would choose yes.

When it's paused for you to absorb information it will wait for a
Carriage return like this. but any key will continue.
                         |
:-(Cr)-: <---------------'

Everything but your pseudonym is hot keyed for maximum play with minimum
effort.  I've duped frequently used keys to the number pad to make it
even easier.

Commands at the main prompt are:

2468 | to your hunger and thirst depending on the terrain you travel on.

  M  | Map of where you've been, marked for you as you travel, you're the *
-----+----------------------------------------------------------------------
 L * | Look ahead, you will be asked for what direction to look: 2468-NEWS
-----+----------------------------------------------------------------------

-----+----------------------------------------------------------------------
 U 0 | Use an item in your bag/hand, some only once, some all the time.
-----+----------------------------------------------------------------------
 G + | Grab an item you find on the ground and place it in your hand or bag
     | TIP: if you were lucky enough to use your swiss army knife first.
-----+----------------------------------------------------------------------
 D - | Drop an item from your bag/hand onto the ground.
-----+----------------------------------------------------------------------
 H   | This selects the item to whack others with from your bag/hands
-----+----------------------------------------------------------------------
 V . | The jungle express, must use to move when tree bound or take the fall
-----+----------------------------------------------------------------------
  P  | View, edit or change the command to execute first in your script
-----+----------------------------------------------------------------------

-----+----------------------------------------------------------------------

  Q  | Put your bwana to sleep until another player rousts them.

SUICIDE
You may take your player out of the game by simply crawling to any edge of the
map and throwing yourself off the cliff.

DEATH
Is not guaranteed except by the cliff at the maps edge, you may go suffering
for a while, when your character dies or is killed while on script you will
have to wait while the buzzards and insects feast.  If only passed out then
strength is regained by sleeping at 300 an hour with a max of 2400.

MAP
The map is very sketchy, the details are filled in as you find them,
remember the toll taken on your character depends upon their health and
the terrain they travel on.

GOALS
Somewhere in the jungle, it's spirit won't allow it to be buried, is the lost
amulet of ganaysha the elephant god.  Once a player finds and puts it on it
can only be removed by death and will be shown to others when they run into
the wearer.  You must wear it, befriend the spirit of ganaysha who appears
in the form of an old elephant.  You must find and feed a sickly young chimp.
Once you have all three of these goals, amulet, power of elephant call, chimp
sidekick, and only then you must travel to the lost temple (marked with an m
on the map) and there take your rightful place upon the throne to be thrown in
the other bwanas face every time they log in and if strong enough at the
moment of coronation upon reaching the temple qualify for top bwanas of all
time. There is a lot more to this game than I can divulge here, you'll just
have to discover it on your own.

                    So go forth and conquer you savage!
